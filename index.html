4. Case Study: Medusa Ransomware Attack

4.1 Summary

Medusa is a ransomware group known for targeting institutions and threatening to leak stolen data unless a ransom is paid. In a 2023 attack, they compromised a hospital’s systems, encrypting patient records and demanding millions in cryptocurrency.

4.2 Ethical Issues

This case presents several ethical concerns: violation of patient privacy, exploitation for profit, and deliberate disruption of critical healthcare services.

4.3 Application of Ethical Theories

From a deontological perspective, Medusa’s actions are inherently unethical regardless of the outcome, as they violate basic duties of respect and non-maleficence. Virtue ethics would highlight the lack of moral character, empathy, and integrity in such acts.

4.4 Ethical Solutions

Stronger cybersecurity policies, ethical hacking education, and international cooperation to track cybercriminals are key solutions. Promoting ethical awareness in tech development can also reduce vulnerabilities exploited by such attacks.

5. Professional Ethics Pledge

As an aspiring IT professional, I solemnly pledge to:

  • Respect the privacy and data of all users I serve.
  • Design and code with honesty, integrity, and fairness.
  • Use technology to empower, not exploit.
  • Report unethical behavior and stand against digital injustice.
  • Continuously educate myself on responsible tech practices.

These principles matter to me because technology influences millions of lives. I want to ensure my work leaves a positive and ethical footprint in the digital world.
